The Scroll

You'll meet someone, you know, and immediately it just works, and that person will convince you Delhi girls aren’t the worst. They don’t make you second-guess you or wonder if there's better out there. You don’t like them for their words, their smile, or their hair. You like them just for existing, existing and strolling until our knees give in, until our lungs can take no more opposite to the godforsaken cath lab. The giggle that erupts from your chest, and you know there's no better because to you, they’re what's best. They text you first, a novel concept I know, and every time you see them, you say, I don't want you to go. They’re kind and forgiving. They take accountability. I'm sorry for what I said. They don’t make fun or pressure you into something. They make me ask what's wrong again, even after they keep saying it's nothing and kick you in the nuts. They exist, in their unadulterated form, even if you haven't found it yet. Their energy transcends and dictates time, and it makes you not want to leave because their heart is worth the climb. They'll make you think you're ethereal, because deep down they know you deserve it all, and you'll know it too, because someday, you'll meet ( I think I’ve) someone that loves just exactly like you.

And that person is you.
